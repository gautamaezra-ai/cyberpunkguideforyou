# Cyberpunk 2077: Full Storyline Guide

Welcome to the ultimate storyline guide for *Cyberpunk 2077*. This guide breaks down the massive, intertwining narrative of V, a mercenary in Night City, and Johnny Silverhand, the digital ghost haunting their mind. 

To keep this guide concise, engaging, and easy to read, we will focus on the main narrative arcs, crucial side characters, and the branching endings. 

---

## Chapter 1: The Lifepaths (The Beginning)

Every legend in Night City has to start somewhere. Before the main events of the game, you choose V's background, known as a "Lifepath." While these paths eventually converge into the same main storyline, they define V's initial worldview, prologue, and dialogue options throughout the game.

### The Nomad
You start in the Badlands, the sprawling, arid desert surrounding Night City. You've recently left your Nomad clan (the Bakkers) and are looking to make a fresh start as a lone wolf. You meet Jackie Welles, a smuggler who hired you to transport a piece of Arasaka corporate cargo across the Night City border. After a tense encounter with border security and a high-speed chase with corporate agents, you and Jackie hide out in a garage, open the cargo (a rare iguana), and bond over the adrenaline rush. You become partners.

### The Streetkid
You are a child of Heywood, raised on the neon-lit streets of Night City. After returning from a failed stint in Atlanta, you take a job from a local fixer, Padre, to steal a rare aerondight car belonging to a high-ranking corporate executive. While breaking into the car, you are held up by Jackie Welles, who is also trying to steal the exact same car. Before you can fight, the Night City Police Department (NCPD) swoops in, backed by a vicious Arasaka inspector. You both take a severe beating but are let go. Bruised but alive, you and Jackie grab a meal and decide to team up.

### The Corpo
You begin at the top of the food chain as an Arasaka counter-intelligence agent. Your boss, Arthur Jenkins, tasks you with assassinating his rival, Susan Abernathy. Jenkins gives you a massive stack of untraceable cash and data to get the job done. You meet your longtime friend and trusted confidant, Jackie Welles, at Lizzie's Bar to plan the hit. However, Abernathy's agents intercept you, strip you of your corporate cyberware, drain your bank accounts, and fire you. Left with absolutely nothing, Jackie steps in, saving your life and offering you a path as an independent mercenary.

No matter which path you choose, a time-skip montage plays, showing V and Jackie moving up the ranks of the Night City underworld, making connections, buying gear, and becoming a formidable mercenary duo.

---

## Chapter 2: The Heist of the Century

V and Jackie finally hit the big leagues when they are contracted by Dexter "Dex" DeShawn, a legendary fixer who has recently returned to Night City. Dex offers them a job of a lifetime: steal an experimental piece of bio-technology called the "Relic" directly from Arasaka Corporation. 

### The Setup
To pull off the heist, V is introduced to Evelyn Parker, the mysterious client who originally commissioned the job, and Judy Alvarez, a brilliant braindance (BD) editor. Judy helps V scrub through Evelyn’s memories of Yorinobu Arasaka’s penthouse to locate the Relic. Evelyn also secretly offers to cut Dex out of the deal, giving V a choice of loyalty.

V and Jackie also acquire a Flathead—an experimental combat bot—from a volatile gang called Maelstrom, navigating a tense standoff between the gang's psychotic leader, Royce, and a Militech corporate agent, Meredith Stout.

### The Infiltration
With the intel gathered and the Flathead in hand, V and Jackie enter Konpeki Plaza, a premier luxury hotel, disguised as corporate arms dealers. They successfully use the Flathead to disable the hotel's security networks and infiltrate Yorinobu Arasaka's penthouse. They secure the Relic, which is housed in a specialized, temperature-controlled case.

### The Tragedy
Before they can escape, Yorinobu arrives at the penthouse unexpectedly, accompanied by his father—the emperor of Arasaka, Saburo Arasaka. V and Jackie hide inside a structural pillar and watch in horror as an argument ensues between father and son. In a fit of rage, Yorinobu strangles Saburo to death. 

Yorinobu immediately initiates a lockdown, claiming his father was poisoned. V and Jackie must shoot their way out of the high-security hotel. During the escape, the Relic’s containment case is damaged. To prevent the organic biochip from degrading, Jackie slots the Relic into his own neural port. 

Tragically, Jackie is mortally wounded during a firefight with an Arasaka gunship. As they escape in an armored Delamain taxi, Jackie bleeds out. With his dying breath, he pulls the Relic from his head and slots it into V’s port. 

### The Betrayal
Traumatized and covered in their best friend's blood, V returns to the No-Tell Motel to meet Dex. Terrified by the fact that they are now framed for the murder of Saburo Arasaka, Dex panics. He betrays V, shoots them in the head, and dumps their body in a landfill to sever his ties to the crime.

---

## Chapter 3: The Ghost in the Shell

V wakes up in a landfill, crawling out of the garbage, barely alive. Dex is there, but he is swiftly executed by Goro Takemura, Saburo Arasaka’s fiercely loyal former bodyguard. Takemura realizes V is a crucial witness to Saburo's true murder and takes them captive to bring to Yorinobu. However, Yorinobu sends corporate assassins to eliminate them both. Takemura and V are forced into a desperate alliance, surviving a brutal car chase.

Takemura brings a fading V to Viktor Vector, a trusted ripperdoc. Viktor manages to stabilize V, but delivers a grim diagnosis: the bullet Dex fired into V's brain should have been fatal. However, it activated the Relic. 

### Enter Johnny Silverhand
The Relic is not just a data drive; it is a prototype "Secure Your Soul" biochip containing an engram—a digitized consciousness. The consciousness belongs to Johnny Silverhand, an infamous rockerboy and anti-corporate terrorist who died in 2023 after detonating a nuclear bomb in the Arasaka Tower. 

The biochip's nanites are actively repairing V's brain, but they are doing so by rewriting V's neural pathways to match Johnny's. V has only a few weeks to live before Johnny’s consciousness completely overwrites their own. 

Johnny appears to V as a hallucination that only V can see and hear. Initially, Johnny is hostile, urging V to kill themselves so he can take over. Over time, as they are forced to share a body and experience each other's memories and emotions, they develop a complex, symbiotic relationship.

To survive, V must find a way to safely remove the Relic. This sends V on three massive, branching main questlines to find leads: tracking down Evelyn Parker, tracking down the Relic's creator, and helping Takemura reach Hanako Arasaka.

---

## Chapter 4: Down the Rabbit Hole (The Evelyn Questline)

V's first lead is Evelyn Parker, the woman who hired them for the heist. If anyone knows who originally wanted the Relic and why, it's her. 

### Clouds and Woodman
V returns to Lizzie's Bar and speaks to Judy Alvarez. Judy reveals Evelyn worked at a dollhouse (a high-end brothel where workers are temporarily chipped with new personalities) called Clouds. V infiltrates Clouds and interrogates the ruthless manager, Woodman, discovering that Evelyn was targeted by a brutal Netrunner attack during a gig and was sold off to a back-alley ripperdoc named Fingers.

### The Scavengers and Disasterpiece
With Judy’s help, V interrogates Fingers, who reveals he sold a comatose Evelyn to Scavengers to be used in illegal, highly illicit braindances (XBDs). V and Judy track the XBD studio to a derelict power plant, slaughtering the Scavs and rescuing Evelyn. However, Evelyn is catatonic, her mind shattered by trauma and the neural attack. 

### The Voodoo Boys
Desperate for answers, V dives into Evelyn’s neural data. They discover Evelyn was hired by the Voodoo Boys, a secretive and highly dangerous gang of netrunners based in Pacifica. 

V contacts Placide, a Voodoo Boys lieutenant, who uses V as a pawn to clear out a rival gang (the Animals) from a local mall. After V survives Placide's betrayal, they meet the gang's leader, Brigitte. Brigitte takes V deep into cyberspace to contact Alt Cunningham—a legendary netrunner, Johnny Silverhand’s former lover, and the creator of the original Soulkiller program (the tech behind the Relic). Alt is no longer human; she is a rogue AI living beyond the Blackwall. 

Alt agrees to help separate V and Johnny’s minds, but she requires access to Mikoshi, Arasaka’s highly secure server fortress where digital souls are stored. The only way to access Mikoshi is to physically infiltrate Arasaka Tower.

*(Tragically, during this questline, Evelyn Parker commits suicide in Judy's apartment, unable to cope with her trauma. This kicks off a lengthy and emotional side questline where V helps Judy liberate Clouds.)*

---

## Chapter 5: Ghost Town (The Hellman Questline)

Simultaneously, V pursues Anders Hellman, the Arasaka bioengineer who designed the Relic. Hellman recently defected from Arasaka and went into hiding. 

### Enter Panam Palmer
To find Hellman, V meets Rogue, the queen of Fixers in Night City and Johnny Silverhand's former partner-in-crime. Rogue demands payment for the intel and points V toward Panam Palmer, an exiled Nomad from the Aldecaldo clan who recently had her car and cargo stolen. 

V helps Panam retrieve her vehicle and get revenge on the man who crossed her. In return, Panam agrees to help V take down a Kang Tao corporate transport AV (Aerial Vehicle) that is secretly extracting Hellman from the city.

### The Badlands Ambush
V and Panam use an EMP to shoot down the AV over the Badlands. A massive firefight ensues against heavily armed Kang Tao operatives. V eventually corners Hellman in an abandoned gas station. 

Unfortunately, Hellman delivers bad news. He cannot fix V. The Relic is functioning exactly as designed; it is a one-way process. The only useful information Hellman provides is the complete blueprints of the Relic, confirming that the change is irreversible by conventional science. 

*(This questline opens up Panam's side quests, where V can help her reclaim her place in the Aldecaldo clan, secure experimental military hardware, and cement the Nomads as powerful, loyal allies.)*

---

## Chapter 6: Play It Safe (The Takemura Questline)

While pursuing Evelyn and Hellman, V also works with Goro Takemura, who is desperate to prove to the Arasaka family that Yorinobu murdered Saburo. 

### The Parade
Takemura’s plan is dangerously bold: he wants to kidnap Hanako Arasaka, Yorinobu’s sister, during a massive, heavily guarded corporate parade in Japantown. Takemura believes Hanako is reasonable and will listen to the truth.

V infiltrates the parade route, systematically eliminating Arasaka snipers and neutralizing Sandayu Oda, Hanako’s personal cyber-ninja bodyguard. With the defenses down, Takemura successfully breaches Hanako's float and extracts her to a safehouse.

### The Safehouse Siege
At the safehouse, V and Takemura attempt to reason with Hanako. Before she can make a decision, an Arasaka strike team, sent by Yorinobu, blows up the building. The floor collapses, separating V from Takemura. 

*(At this point, V has a hidden choice. The game objective tells V to run, but V can choose to fight back through the collapsing building to save Takemura's life. Saving him alters the game's final act.)*

V escapes the chaos but passes out from the Relic's degradation, waking up outside the city. A proxy doll delivers a message: Hanako believes V and wants to meet in secret at Embers, a high-end restaurant. 

---

## Chapter 7: Phantom Liberty (The Expansion Interlude)

*If you own the Phantom Liberty expansion, the story integrates here.* V is contacted by Songbird, an elite netrunner for the New United States of America (NUSA). Songbird claims she can cure V's condition, but in exchange, V must rescue Rosalind Myers, the President of the NUSA, whose orbital shuttle has crashed in Dogtown—a walled-off, lawless district of Night City ruled by a warlord named Kurt Hansen.

V rescues the President and is sworn into the Federal Intelligence Agency (FIA) as a secret agent. V teams up with Solomon Reed (a sleeper agent played by Idris Elba) to track down Songbird, who was captured during the crash. 

The expansion is a sprawling spy-thriller filled with espionage, betrayals, and political intrigue. V discovers that Songbird is dying from exposure to rogue AIs beyond the Blackwall and that the promised "cure" (a highly advanced Neural Matrix) can only be used once. 

V is forced to make a devastating choice: 
1. **Betray Reed** and help Songbird escape to the moon so she can use the cure. 
2. **Betray Songbird**, leading to a terrifying horror-survival sequence where V must capture her for the NUSA. 

Depending on your choices, *Phantom Liberty* can unlock a brand-new ending to the main game (The Tower ending), where NUSA surgeons successfully extract the Relic, but V falls into a two-year coma, loses the ability to use combat cyberware, and awakens to find their friends have moved on, leaving V as a face in the crowd.

---

## Chapter 8: Nocturne Op55N1 (The Point of No Return)

V meets Hanako Arasaka at Embers. Hanako reveals she knew her brother killed their father all along, but she chose stability over truth. Now, Yorinobu is actively trying to dismantle Arasaka from the inside, and she wants V to help her stage a corporate coup. In exchange, she offers access to Mikoshi to save V's life. 

Following the meeting, V collapses as the Relic reaches its final stages. V wakes up in Viktor's clinic. Viktor gives V pills to suppress Johnny, a gun, and tells V they have mere hours to live. 

V goes to the rooftop above Misty's Esoterica with Johnny. Sitting on the ledge looking over Night City, V must make the final, game-defining decision on how to assault Arasaka Tower to reach Mikoshi. 

---

## Chapter 9: The Endings

The culmination of *Cyberpunk 2077* changes drastically based on the side quests V completed and the choice made on the rooftop.

### 1. The Devil (Hanako's Path)
V accepts Hanako’s offer. You infiltrate Arasaka Tower to capture Yorinobu. You discover that Saburo Arasaka is not entirely dead; his consciousness was saved on an engram. Saburo’s engram is forcefully injected into Yorinobu’s body, resurrecting the Emperor. 
V is taken to an orbital Arasaka medical facility. The Relic is removed, but the damage to V's DNA is terminal; they will die in six months. V is offered a terrifying choice: return to Earth to die in a few months, or sign their soul away to Arasaka, having their mind turned into an engram and stored in Mikoshi until a new body can be found. It is a bleak, corporate-dominated ending.

### 2. The Star (The Nomad Path)
*Requires completing Panam's side quests.* V calls Panam and the Aldecaldos for help. The Nomads launch a massive, full-scale military assault on Arasaka Tower, using a stolen Basilisk hover-tank. Several Nomads, including Saul, die in the assault. V reaches Mikoshi and connects to Alt Cunningham. Alt separates V and Johnny, but reveals V's body will still reject V's mind in six months. Johnny voluntarily stays in cyberspace with Alt. V wakes up and leaves Night City forever with Panam, Judy (if romanced), and the Aldecaldos, determined to spend their remaining months free and looking for a cure in the desert. This is widely considered the most hopeful and "good" ending.

### 3. The Sun (The Rogue Path)
*Requires completing Johnny and Rogue's side quests.*
V takes the suppression pills, giving Johnny complete control of their body. Johnny and Rogue storm Arasaka Tower. Rogue sacrifices herself to kill Adam Smasher (the cyborg who killed Johnny in 2023). Johnny reaches Mikoshi and connects to Alt. In cyberspace, Johnny honors his promise, returning the body to V and walking beyond the Blackwall. V awakens as a living legend in Night City, taking over the Afterlife. Facing inevitable death, V takes on one final, impossible heist: an assault on the Crystal Palace orbital casino, going out in a blaze of glory.

### 4. Temperance (Johnny Keeps the Body)
This can happen in either the Nomad or Rogue path. In Mikoshi, V chooses to cross the Blackwall with Alt, effectively dying/evolving into an AI, and allows Johnny Silverhand to keep V's body permanently. Johnny returns to the real world, deeply changed by V's sacrifice. He buys a guitar for a young neighbor, visits V's grave to pay his respects, and quietly leaves Night City on a bus to start a new, quiet life, leaving his rebellious past behind.

### 5. Path of Glory (The Secret Ending)
*Requires specific dialogue choices during Johnny's grave side quest and waiting 5 real-life minutes on the rooftop.*
V decides not to risk anyone else's life. Armed to the teeth, V (with Johnny cheering them on) walks through the front door of Arasaka Tower entirely alone. It is a suicide mission with no checkpoints; if V dies, the game ends. If V succeeds in slaughtering through the entire Arasaka army and Adam Smasher, they reach Mikoshi. The outcome plays out similarly to "The Sun" ending, but Rogue survives, and V is cemented as the ultimate, undisputed legend of Night City.

### 6. The Easy Way Out
On the rooftop, V decides that no more blood needs to be shed. V tosses the pills over the ledge and commits suicide. The game cuts to black, and the credits roll, showing heartbroken video messages from V's friends reacting to their sudden, tragic choice.

---

## Conclusion
*Cyberpunk 2077* is a story about legacy, survival, and the cost of ambition in a hyper-capitalist dystopia. Whether V fades away quietly, burns out in a blaze of glory, or finds peace in the badlands, their journey leaves an indelible mark on Night City and the ghost that shared their mind.
